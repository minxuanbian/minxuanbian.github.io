30,000 Days (Wrinkle, Wrinkle, Little Star)

Site-adaptable performance

This project explores the subtle distortions of time in human life — its folds, its repetitions, and its quiet dissolving edges.

Inspired by the lived experience of Alzheimer’s disease, the work approaches memory not as a linear narrative, but as something that drifts, overlaps, and reappears without warning. Fragments of the past emerge like a light drizzle — unfinished, fragile, yet persistent.

At its center is a final day that is both ordinary and irreversible. Within this blurred temporality, the desire to say goodbye becomes urgent yet impossible to complete.

The work asks:

How do we say goodbye to a life that is already slipping away?
And how do we recognize the ones who have been our light, when time itself begins to forget?
