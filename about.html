Minxuan Bian

Minxuan Bian is a theatre director working between perception, memory, and relational space.

Her work focuses on unstable temporalities — how time folds, dissolves, and reappears through the body. She is particularly interested in moments where reality becomes uncertain: memory loss, misrecognition, and fragile trust between individuals.

Rather than constructing fixed narratives, her directing practice builds situations — where performers and audience coexist within shifting conditions. These situations often invite hesitation, silence, and incompletion as core elements of the work.

Her recent works explore themes including Alzheimer's disease, collaborative limits, and the weight of individual agency within collective structures.

She has worked with artists including Robert Wilson and Pippo Delbono, whose practices have influenced her sensitivity to structure, rhythm, and presence.
